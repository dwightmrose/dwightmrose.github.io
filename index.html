About Me

Born in Jamaica and raised with purpose, I discovered storytelling as a child — building worlds out of cardboard and dreams. With a BFA in Theater from University at Buffalo and a degree in Entertainment Technology, I embraced the stage, mastering carpentry on Disney sets and choreographing emotion in contemporary dance. Off‑Broadway projects and indie films taught me vulnerability; every role, from a grieving son in “Almost Maine” to a tormented George in “Who’s Afraid of Virginia Woolf?”, became a journey of truth.

My voice carries into character — from theatrical monologues to voice-over reels — each note grounded in authenticity and raw, Jamaican soul.
